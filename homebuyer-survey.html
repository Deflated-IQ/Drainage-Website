Why You Need One

Why Get a Homebuyer Drain Survey?

A standard homebuyer survey or mortgage valuation does not include an inspection of the drainage system. This means you could unknowingly buy a property with thousands of pounds worth of drain defects — and only discover them after you've moved in.

💡 Did you know? In Oxford's Victorian and Edwardian properties, original clay drainage pipes are extremely common — and many have never been surveyed. Cracked pipes, root intrusion and collapsed sections are frequently found.

Our homebuyer drain survey gives you:

  • Full HD CCTV footage of the drain and sewer system
  • Written drainage survey report accepted by solicitors
  • Clear condition rating for each section of drain
  • Repair recommendations and estimated costs if needed
  • Negotiating power if defects are found
  • Peace of mind before you commit

What We Check in a Homebuyer Survey

📷

All Private Drainage Runs

We camera-survey all drain runs on the property from access points and inspection chambers.

🔵

Inspection Chambers & Manholes

All manholes and inspection chambers are accessed and checked for condition, connections and correct flows.

🌿

Root Intrusion

We check for tree roots entering the drain — extremely common in Oxford's older properties with mature garden trees.

💔

Pipe Condition

All pipe joints, cracks, fractures and pipe material are recorded and assessed for their current condition and longevity.

🔗

Drainage Connections

We verify correct connections between foul and surface water drains, identifying any illegal or incorrectly connected drainage.
