Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about drainage in Oxford and Oxfordshire — answered by our local engineers.

In the UK, responsibility is split between the homeowner and the local water authority (typically Thames Water for the Oxfordshire area):

  • Homeowner: You are responsible for drains within your property boundary that serve only your home.
  • Thames Water: They are responsible for "lateral drains" (pipes outside your boundary) and shared public sewers.

Tip: If your neighbour's drains are also backing up, it's likely a shared sewer issue — contact your water provider first.

Early detection can save you from a costly repair. Look out for:

  • Gurgling sounds coming from your sinks or toilets
  • Foul smells near manhole covers or internal plug holes
  • Slow drainage in the shower, bath, or kitchen sink
  • Rising water levels in the toilet after flushing

For minor sink or toilet clogs, a plunger or eco-friendly enzyme cleaner may work. However, if an outside drain is overflowing, we strongly advise against DIY rodding — it can push the blockage further down or damage the pipework.
